What Is Skool?

A community platform built by someone who actually understood the problem

Skool was built by Sam Ovens — a New Zealand entrepreneur who ran multi-million dollar coaching businesses and kept hitting the same wall: community platforms were either too complex or too passive. Skool vs Kajabi

Kajabi is the most common comparison and the answer genuinely depends on what you're building. If your primary product is a community where people interact, engage, and support each other, Skool wins clearly — its community feed and gamification produce engagement rates that Kajabi's community feature simply can't match. If you're primarily selling standalone courses with sophisticated email funnels and sales pages, Kajabi's marketing automation tools are stronger. Skool vs Teachable

These two don't really compete. Teachable is a pure course platform with no community whatsoever. If you want students talking to each other, helping each other, and staying engaged long-term, Teachable has nothing to offer. Skool is the clear choice for anyone who wants community as part of the product. Teachable is only the better choice if you want to sell a simple video course to someone, deliver it, and have no ongoing relationship with them.

Skool vs Circle

Circle is the closest genuine competitor in terms of positioning. Both are community-first platforms at similar price points. Skool wins on simplicity and gamification. Circle wins on customisation and white-labelling. For most creators, Skool's simplicity is a feature — the cleaner interface means better member retention. Circle's additional customisation options come with additional complexity that most people never need.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Skool cost?
Skool costs $99 per month flat, regardless of how many members you have. There are no transaction fees, no tiered pricing, and no hidden costs. You pay standard Stripe payment processing fees on member subscriptions, but Skool itself takes nothing from your revenue.
Does Skool have a free trial?
Yes — Skool offers a 14-day free trial with full access to all features. No credit card is required to start. You can build your community, upload courses, and invite members during the trial period before deciding whether to subscribe.
Is Skool better than Kajabi?
Skool is better than Kajabi for community-first creators. Kajabi has stronger marketing automation and email tools, but charges significantly more ($149-$399/month) and its community features are basic. If your core product is a paid community with courses, Skool wins. If you need sophisticated sales funnels and email sequences built in, Kajabi is more complete — though most creators end up pairing Skool with a standalone email tool like Kit.
Can you make money with Skool?
Yes, in two ways. First, by charging members for access to your community — you keep all revenue minus Stripe's standard processing fees. Second, through Skool's affiliate programme, which pays 40% recurring monthly commission for every person you refer to the platform, for life. Several creators earn more from Skool affiliate commissions than from their own communities.
Is Skool worth it for beginners?
Skool is worth it for beginners who already have some kind of audience — a social following, a newsletter, a YouTube channel — that they can invite to their community. The $99/month fee requires roughly 5-10 paying members at $20/month to break even. Without any existing audience, it's worth using Skool's free community tier while building an audience first before moving to a paid community.
Skool vs Teachable — which is better?
Skool and Teachable serve different needs. Teachable is a pure course platform with no community features — you upload a course, students buy it, they watch it, done. Skool combines community, courses, and events so that members interact, help each other, and stay engaged. If you want students talking to each other and building relationships, Skool is clearly better. If you simply want to sell a standalone video course with no ongoing community, Teachable is simpler.
Does Skool take a percentage of revenue?
No. Skool charges a flat $99/month and does not take any cut of your membership revenue. You pay Stripe's standard payment processing fees (approximately 2.9% + 30p per transaction) but Skool takes nothing from your sales. This is a meaningful advantage over platforms like Mighty Networks which takes 3% of revenue.
Who created Skool?
Skool was founded by Sam Ovens, a New Zealand entrepreneur known for building multi-million dollar online coaching businesses. He created Skool to solve the community engagement problem he kept hitting on other platforms — the combination of a social feed, courses, and gamification in one place was his solution to low engagement and high churn that plagued other tools.
What are the best alternatives to Skool?
The best alternatives to Skool are: Circle.so (community-focused, similar price point, more customisable), Kajabi (full marketing suite, higher price), Teachable (course-only, no community), Mighty Networks (older platform, more complex interface), and Discord (free but no monetisation tools). For most creators wanting community plus courses in one clean platform, Skool remains the strongest option in 2026.
How does Skool's affiliate programme work?
Skool's affiliate programme pays 40% recurring commission for life on the $99/month subscription — that's $39.60 per month per referral. You sign up at skool.com/affiliate-program, get a unique link, and earn every month that your referrals stay subscribed. Commissions are held for 14 days then released. Payouts go directly to your bank account. There's no cap on earnings and no minimum referral requirement to join.
